💡 Classical Bits vs ⚛️ Quantum Qubits

Imagine teaching computers using light bulbs. A normal computer uses a simple switch. A quantum computer uses something much more mysterious.

Designed to help students understand the idea visually

🖥️ Classical Computer

A classical computer stores information as Bits.

Bit = 0 or 1

State: 0 (OFF)
Explanation:

Just like a room light switch:

❌ OFF → Bit = 0
✅ ON → Bit = 1

It cannot be both at the same time.

⚛️ Quantum Computer

A quantum computer stores information as Qubits.

Qubit = Blend of 0 and 1

50% |0⟩ + 50% |1⟩
Explanation:

Unlike a normal bit, a qubit can exist in a superposition.

The slider is a teaching analogy:

🌑 Mostly 0
🌗 Combination of 0 and 1
🌕 Mostly 1

When measured, the qubit collapses into either 0 or 1.

🧠 What Should Students Remember?

Bit

Only one state at a time.
0 OR 1

Qubit

Can be a combination.
0 AND 1

Measurement

Observing the qubit forces it into a definite answer.

⚠️ The dimmer bulb is an analogy. Real qubits are governed by the strange rules of quantum mechanics, but this visualization helps beginners build intuition.
